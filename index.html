What nobody had told her

On a Tuesday afternoon, my friend Susan sent me a link with a simple message: "Read this. Seriously."

It was about what actually happens to sleep after 45. And for the first time in years, someone was explaining my specific situation — not insomnia in general. Not "try chamomile tea."

After 45, sleep breaks down for three reasons that compound each other:

  • Estrogen regulates your internal thermostat. When it fluctuates, your body loses its ability to cool down naturally at night. Hot flashes, night sweats, and that sticky, overheated feeling aren't random — they're a physiological thermoregulation failure. (Source: NIH Sleep Foundation)
  • Progesterone has a calming effect on the nervous system. As it declines, your brain becomes more reactive at night. Easier to wake. Harder to fall back asleep. Sleep becomes light and fragmented even when you're exhausted.
  • Your circadian rhythm loses precision. The internal clock drifts. You wake at the same time every night, feel sleepy at strange moments, or sleep for seven hours and still feel wrecked.

These three things sabotage each other. Fixing just one doesn't work. That's why the generic advice never lasts more than a few days.
